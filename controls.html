Controls & Mechatronics Projects

Introduction to Feedback Control Course

In this course, I learned how to design PID, State-Space, and Frequency-Domain Controllers on a variety of mechanical systems, both simulated and physical.

The video to the left shows a simulation of a VTOL aircraft with an Integral State Feedback Controller that I designed. This selection of controller has all the advantages of state-space control, with the added benefit of integral action to eliminate steady-state error.

Key skills: Python, control design, system dynamics, git.

Soft Robotics Research: BYU RAD Lab

While working in the BYU Robotics and Dynamics (RAD) Lab, I assisted in creating learned dynamics models of soft robot arms. I supported these efforts by:

1. Performing system identification on the single link arm you see in the video as a starting point for training.
2. Optimizing training hyperparameters (number of epochs, amount of data).
3. Collecting 4 hours worth of data of joint and pressure commands.

Key skills: Python, system identification, machine learning, ROS, git, Linux.

Mechatronics Course: Mars Rover Robot

For my mechatronics class, my team built an autonomous robot to navigate a modular course simulating Mars rover tasks. The course's dynamic nature required a robust algorithm capable of handling any task order.

I contributed heavily to both the embedded programming and electrical integration, developing the state-machine-based algorithm and wiring the various sensors and components (stepper motors, servo motors, IR sensors, voltage regulators, and ultrasonic sensors, QRDs, etc).

Key skills: embedded programming (C), electronics, debugging.

Mechatronics Club Competition: Color Sorting Robot

This was a fun little competition to see who could sort 40 colored ping pong balls the fastest. My partner and I won 1st place by sorting 40 ping pong balls in 41 seconds. Yes—it is made exclusively from cardboard and hot glue—but it was budget friendly and it got the job done! The video shows snippets from the competition as well as an explanation of how it works.

Key skills: embedded programming (C/Arduino), basic electronics, debugging.
